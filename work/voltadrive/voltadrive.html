VoltaDrive

this redesign calls for a rebrand.
About

VoltaDrive gives you a car to drive for services like Uber and Lyft, similar to Breeze. If you choose to drive Uber, you'll get the option to drive UberBlack, making 3x more than the average Lyft driver.

Challenge

When voltadrive first approached me, there were no prior resources-photos, guides, informative website. I had to start from scratch, with client input at every step. A design nightmare.

Role

I needed to create a website from branding, to design, to code. I decided to learn bootstrap in a few hours to quicken the code process where the main goal is to have users sign up.

Find the Problem

Start Wireframing

Creating boxes on sketch tells me the "must-haves" in the website. I then rearrange the information so that it flows more smoothly, with of course, the CTA button in mind.

Change.

I used the same resources from the actual website- font, color, & photos. Using the exact same resources, I created a more intuitive rearrangement. The message is now more effective, and the design is more aesthetically appealing as well.

Difference.

Because more people are using their phones than ever (67%), it's very important to have a mobile first approach. Simple rearranging and intuitive color variations can really make a difference.

Current Page


Problem.

Just like every UX designer says, every step should have meaning that helps the problem. Here, the client needed to display enough convincing information to have users sign up.

Solution.

What does it take for someone to sign up? Just like asking someone for their number, you'll first have to 1. give them your name, 2. talk about yourself 3. why they should give it to you 4.why you're special 5.what they'll get out of it. Oh, and let's not forget being sleek.

That' a Wrap.

Strategizing content first through questionnaires + wireframes not only makes sure you're not distracting by design's mesmerizing beauty, but makes it have a functional purpose. With VoltaDrive, users know exactly what they're signing up for, and why they SHOULD. It also helps to get the information between designer and client as clear as possible.

About

Imperfect's mission is to take "ugly foods" rejected in stores, and deliver them to you for cheaper. They're on a mission to fight food waste.

Challenge

Their current website did not implement basic design principles. Information everywhere, difficult to understand and read, lack aesthetics.

Role

I wanted to make a simple redesign, using the exact same resources that they used to not only create a better looking layout, but also optimized for functionality. Just like how things should be.

Visit actual site →

Question

How do I make this mobile site better?

Anything can always be better. Improved. As long as change continues.




Hypothesis: Lean Design

If I can create a lean design by using the same resources and rearrange them in a more optimal way, conversions should go up.

Resources are only as good as someone's ability to use them.

Cover Redesign

This is the first thing users see, and it should be good.


Change.

I used the same resources from the actual website- font, color, & photos. Using the exact same resources, I created a more intuitive rearrangement. The message is now more effective, and the design is more aesthetically appealing as well.

Difference.

Because more people are using their phones than ever (67%), it's very important to have a mobile first approach. Simple rearranging and intuitive color variations can really make a difference.

Mission Statement Redesign

You get to tell people what you do and how you do it. This should be very clear and concise.

Current Page

Problem.

The second page has a very high bounce rate. A list of companies with distracting arrangement tells me nothing about what the company actually does. The page leaves me feeling bored and confused.

What's ugly produce? and what is being delivered?

Redesign


Change.

I placed the most crucial information within the first 2 pages (less scrolling for user). The CTA button is the main purpose of the page, and everything from text, color, content, is aimed to accentuate that fact.

Difference.

Because more people are using their phones than ever (67%), it's very important to have a mobile first approach. Simple rearranging and intuitive color variations can really make a difference.

That' a Wrap.

I used the exact same resources from the original, and made it better. Functional aesthetics is how I like to do things. Optimized functionality + maximum aesthetics always has it's balance. A good UX designer should always be thinking of both. As much as I would love to redeisgn their entire web/mobile user flow, that would not be good business practice.

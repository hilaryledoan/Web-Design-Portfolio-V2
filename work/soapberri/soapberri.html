Building from Scratch

Here's the problem with designing for a company from ground zero: there's no target market, style guide, or prior research. How do you build something out of nothing?

You build a website with the purpose to find your target market

1st website

Find Target Market

The purpose is to find WHO is most interested in our product and WHAT storyline will make them most likely to buy.

We came up with a few different concepts to test:
1. Natural soap-sudding factor
2. Social/Environmental Responsibility
3. Value Props Obsession

Develop Website with Zero Experience

With no skills in design/tech, I had 1 month to learn whatever I needed.

Technical: I picked up HTML/CSS/Jquery so I can start building

Design: I interviewed many designers, went to meet-ups, and read a bunch on UI/UX, design concepts, and learned sketch so I can start wireframing.

Read the casetudy

Numbers don't lie

Results: There was 1 thing consistant across all 40 people: the magical berry lathering experience. A few things that must go into the 2nd iteration: 1. lathering experience 2. value props
